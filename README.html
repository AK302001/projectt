Short Note about APJ Sir

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, known as A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was an Indian scientist, professor, and statesman who served as the 11th President of India. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, he was a key figure in India's civilian space program and military missile development.Dr. Kalam played a crucial role in the success of India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, earning him the moniker "People's President" for his accessible and down-to-earth demeanor during his presidency from 2002 to 2007.

Vision and Mission

Beyond his scientific and political contributions, Abdul Kalam was an inspiring figure, especially for the youth. He emphasized the importance of education, dreams, and hard work. His motivational speeches and writings continue to influence and ignite the aspirations of millions around the world. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, leaving behind a legacy of scientific excellence, leadership, and a deep belief in the power of dreams.

The Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam National Memorial was built in memory of Kalam by the DRDO in Pei Karumbu, in the island town of Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
